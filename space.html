The Big Bang theory is the leading explanation about how the universe began. At its simplest, it talks about the universe as we know it starting with a small singularity, then inflating over the next 13.8 billion years to the cosmos that we know today.

Explore and Observation

Astronomers use telescopes to observe distant objects in space. These telescopes can be ground-based or space-based, and they collect light and other forms of electromagnetic radiation to study celestial bodies. By analyzing the light from stars and galaxies, astronomers can determine their composition, temperature, distance, and motion.

Space missions, such as those conducted by NASA and other space agencies, have provided valuable data about our solar system and beyond. Probes and rovers have been sent to explore planets, moons, and asteroids, sending back images and scientific measurements that help us understand the conditions and history of these celestial bodies.

Modern Space Exploration

Space exploration has entered a new era with the advent of reusable rockets, international collaborations, and advanced robotic missions. Organizations like NASA, ESA, ISRO, and private companies such as SpaceX and Blue Origin are pushing the boundaries of what is possible, from Mars rovers to lunar landers and plans for human missions to Mars.

  • International Space Station (ISS): A symbol of global cooperation, the ISS serves as a microgravity laboratory and a testbed for future deep space missions.
  • Reusable Rockets: Innovations in rocket technology have dramatically reduced the cost of access to space, making frequent launches and satellite deployments more feasible.
  • Space Telescopes: Instruments like Hubble and James Webb Space Telescope allow us to peer deeper into the universe than ever before.
Black Holes: The Cosmic Enigma

Black holes are regions in space where gravity is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape. They are formed from the remnants of massive stars that have ended their life cycles. The study of black holes has revolutionized our understanding of physics, space, and time.

  • Event Horizon: The boundary around a black hole beyond which nothing can return.
  • Supermassive Black Holes: Found at the centers of most galaxies, including our own Milky Way.
  • First Image: In 2019, the Event Horizon Telescope captured the first-ever image of a black hole in the galaxy M87.
James Webb Space Telescope (JWST)

The James Webb Space Telescope is the most powerful space telescope ever built. Launched in 2021, JWST is designed to look further back in time than any previous telescope, studying the formation of the first galaxies, stars, and planets. Its infrared capabilities allow it to peer through cosmic dust and reveal the hidden universe.

  • Infrared Astronomy: JWST observes the universe in infrared, revealing objects invisible to optical telescopes.
  • Origins of Galaxies: JWST helps scientists study the earliest galaxies and the evolution of the cosmos.
  • Exoplanet Atmospheres: The telescope can analyze the atmospheres of planets orbiting other stars, searching for signs of habitability.
